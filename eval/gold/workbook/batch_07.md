## 9 · review 219945861
- [x] reviewed

```text
No Man's Sky game has plenty to offer - procedurally generated planets (including weather, terrain, etc.), animals, plants, encounters, etc. It even has base building, freighters and expeditions, and settlement management. But it's unfortunately a mile wide and an inch deep. It tries to do everything, but doesn't do any one thing better than any other game. 

From the moment you leave the starting planet, the game is an exercise in tedium and repetition. I spent 60 hours in this game. I continuously wondered what the point of all of it was. I was never challenged. I never felt a sense of danger. I never had epic moments of wonder or of adventure. 

I feel like I could forgive its monotony and incredible grind if either: I felt I was in a universe with other people, or the UI wasn't so damn dreadful. 

The multiplayer aspects of this game are totally unnecessary. I never fought off pirates beside a random person, nor had to group up with someone to take on a difficult mission. The moments I did interact with others, it was passing by someone in the corridor of the Anomaly or looking at their ship as they came to land in the same place.  Think of Destiny's Tower system, but with more steps between NPCs. 

And I've been saving the worst part for last: the UI. It is genuinely one of the worst user interfaces I've ever used in a game. Sure, it has a quest log and a compendium to track your progress, it has inventories, it has customisation to your exosuit or ship or your tools. Even the crafting menus exist. But that's all that can be said positively about the UI. 

There are ZERO shortcuts. Nearly EVERYTHING requires you to hold a key down for a second, and everything has an animation that you cannot skip or speed up. And you do this several thousand times in your playthrough. Landing a ship? Yep, you have to watch the same animation everytime. Teleporting to a settlement? Yep, hold TAB to skip the forced window that comes up telling you what planet you're on. Opening a box? Hold on, before you take what's in the box, you have to first aim way above it because the icon conflicts with switching off a random light in the middle of a barren wasteland, and then you hold a button to complete an arbitrary second-long interaction before the animation starts and you have to remove the random junk from the front of it so you can then persevere through another animation and a mandatory 2 second waiting period. THEN you will be given an item that you will ignore for your entire playthrough. 

And the UI constantly overrides itself. If I pin a quest, stop overriding it just because I entered a different star system and got attacked by pirates that was entirely out of my control. If I pin a quest, stop overriding it just because I'm holding a map that I received as a quest reward from my last quest. If I pin a quest, stop overriding it just because I accidentally complete a random side objective by using my mining laser on an animal that accidentally stepped in front of me. If I already have the items in my inventory when I pick up a quest, PLEASE for the love of god, let me hand it in immediately - I don't want to have to teleport or fly offworld just to give the game time to realise that I have what I need in my inventory. And stop giving me quest rewards like recipes/blueprints that I already unlocked because I couldn't be bothered waiting for the quest NPC to recognise I had the items in my inventory all along.

The UI is INCREDIBLY frustrating. I think I spent 18 of my 60 hours in this game in animations I couldn't skip, interactions I couldn't make instantaneous, and dialogue that I couldn't skip immediately. 

Overall, No Man's Sky is fine. Someone will enjoy it. But for me, it was mediocre at best and frustratingly tedious at worst.
```
