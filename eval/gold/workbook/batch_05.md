## 3 · review 225665982
- [x] reviewed

```text
It scratches an itch. Bannerlord have a style of game that could be the sweet spot between Civ style world/kingdom building with a mix of Total War battles. However the execution is verging on poor. 

Vanilla game is okay, its not great. If you want to have any kind of fun then you will absolutely need mods as the base systems and features just arent there. Mods for diplomacy, larger battles and armies are a must.

The positives are; 
- you can play the game multiple different ways (Merc, Trader, Vassel, Ruler) and its completely open as to how you want your character to play out, it doesnt force you into a specific type. 
- The world, for the most part, moves pretty consistantly and things constantly change, kingdoms lose towns and castles and armies declare war on you out of the blue.  
- Battles can be fun and do require some thinking, especially if you find yourself outnumbered. 
- Seiges are a great feature, you just lack the ability to control them well.
- The kingdoms are well thought out and styled and the database that is there could very easily be expanded upon and made to be more interactive.

Cons are; 
- Dialouges are rough and uninteresting, every NPC interaction is identical and repetitive. 
- AI lords and leaders are terrible when it comes to tactics, both in seiges and in battles.
- Wars are persistent and sometimes seem pointlessly invoked and when this happens continuously as you are trying to rebuild or work on other things it came become incredibly frustrating. 
- Graphics are very poor. 
- There is so much potential in the towns and villages if they were more interactive.
- Hideouts are bland and monotonous. 

Overall the potential was huge, but its a basic rendition and for a while it is fun. I would recommend simply for the joy of the first playthrough, after that the cracks in the game start to spoil the overall enjoyment. Buy on discount
```
